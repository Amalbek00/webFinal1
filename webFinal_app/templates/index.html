Mercedes-Benz showcases luxury EV interior of the future

Mercedes-Benz has showcased its latest fully electric concept vehicle, the Vision EQS, designed for an environmentally minded luxury market. According to Mercedes, the interior design takes its inspiration from the world of luxury yachts. For the first time, the entire dashboard blends with the body of the front trim section to form an interior sculpture.

Mercedes-AMG GT 4-Door Coupé

The AMG GT 4-Door Coupé uses a front-engine layout with 4MATIC+ fully variable all-wheel-drive (rear-biased), and is available with either a 3.0 L M256 turbo/supercharged inline-six engine or a 4.0 L M177 twin-turbocharged V8 engine.[5][6] The inline-six engine features a conventional turbocharger as well as a mild hybrid system that Mercedes-Benz calls "EQ Boost", which uses an electrically driven supercharger and a 48 volt electric motor that when combined, produce an extra 21 hp (16 kW) and 184 lb⋅ft (249 N⋅m) of torque in addition to the petrol engine when it is not powering the electrical system.

The Mercedes-AMG GT R Pro Is a Hardcore Trackday Beast

The Mercedes-AMG GT R is a mighty capable machine—indeed, it once held a Nürburgring production car lap record. The GT R Pro, with 585 horsepower, has the same output as the lesser GT R, but it's more of a track dog, thanks to a lighter, more rigid chassis and further aero enhancements.

Mercedes-Benz's Plan for Surviving the Auto Revolution

If you're the guy in charge of leading Daimler—you know, the world’s largest luxury carmaker, one of Europe’s most important tech companies, and the inventor of the automobile—into a threatening future, it can’t hurt to have a name that sounds made for a superhero. Good thing it's a guy named Wilko Stark helming CASE (that’s connectivity, autonomous, shared and services, and electric mobility), which Daimler launched in 2016 to address the most promising and troubling trends under one roof.
